🧠 Brain Sketch

A sketchbook physics puzzle — draw pencil ramps on paper, tap Play, and roll the ball into the glowing star.

Editor's pick: short levels, satisfying strokes, and real gravity — perfect for a two-minute brain break.
▶ Play Brain Sketch now

Who is this for?

Fans of draw-to-solve physics puzzlers who want quick, creative levels without leaving the feed.

How to play

Tap Start on the card cover, then draw on the canvas. Your pencil lines become solid ramps and walls. When you're ready, tap ▶ Play — gravity takes over and the orange ball rolls. Touch the star to clear the level.

Tips

  • Watch the ink meter — gentle curves often beat one long scribble.
  • Use Undo (3 per level) to fix a stroke without clearing everything.
  • Steep ramps can launch the ball off-screen; aim for a smooth slide toward the star.
  • Toggle day/night with ◐ if the paper glare is hard on your eyes.

FAQ

How does ink work?
Each level has a limited ink bar. Longer strokes consume more ink.
What if the ball falls off?
Strokes reset and the ball returns to the start — try a new ramp.
How many levels?
12 puzzles, then the set loops from the beginning.
